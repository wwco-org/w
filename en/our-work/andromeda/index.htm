Our work

Andromeda

Androméda — (ru.Андромéда) — public universal Internet encyclopedia about outer space, implemented on the principles of wiki. Located at andromeda.com. The owner of the site is the Russian non-profit organization WWCO, which has 25 regional offices.

Андромеда started with an idea: to create a free-to-edit encyclopedia of outer space for the whole world and with the help of the whole world. Over the years, we have made significant progress. Millions of members, billions of readers and millions of people have contributed to the development of online education over the years.

People like you keep our projects accurate and objective!

Every day the Atom is constantly growing and developing. Volunteers openly collaborate to test each other's work and ensure accuracy. In order for the content to remain in the Atom, it must be attributed to a reliable source so that readers can check the facts. Bots and monitoring mechanisms help ensure that articles comply with these standards. Most editors act in good faith, but if someone intentionally makes destructive or malicious changes, the community will find and fix them as soon as possible.

Creating content about yourself or your company is considered a direct violation of the CDI Community rules

In most of our projects, people who have failed in Wikimedia Foundation projects can create pages about themselves or their businesses with us - creating such articles is completely prohibited in accordance with our CDI Community rules, we are engaged in online education, and not collecting information about various personalities and their activities.

Atom will never advertise

Atom is dedicated to free, reliable and neutral knowledge. We are not against online advertising, but we will not advertise in WWCO projects. Commercial interests may jeopardize the position of free knowledge as a neutral source of information.

All texts of articles and most media files in WWCO projects can be reused, but:

You can use the text from the WWCO projects article in your book, article or website, but you do not have the right to assign the materials to yourself, and you must also specify the appropriate licenses. The text content of WWCO projects can be used in accordance with the terms of the license GEL'S Special; unless otherwise specified, it can also be used in accordance with the terms of the license GNU Free Documentation License.

Most of the images in WWCO projects are either publicly available or published under two licenses Creative Commons and CDI GEL'S which allows you to reuse them for free. Copyright information for each image is available when you click on it. Be sure to read this information before reusing the media file.
