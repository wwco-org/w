Cookie agreement

Welcome!

As stated in our Privacy Policy, WWCO strongly believes in the values of privacy and transparency. For this purpose, we have created this Cookie Statement as a clear guide to the use of cookies on WWCO sites. This cookie statement explains how we use cookies (and other local storage technologies), how we use third-party cookies, and how you can manage your cookie settings. For more information about our privacy policy, please visit our Privacy Policy.

WorldWide CDI Organization, a non-profit organization that hosts WWCO projects, actively collects some types of information using various widely used technologies. These typically include tracking pixels, JavaScript, and various "locally stored data" technologies such as cookies and local storage.

What is a cookie?
A cookie is a tiny data file that we transmit to your computer, mobile phone or any other device that you use to access WWCO sites, and is usually used for authentication and tracking. Each cookie expires after a certain period of time, but this period varies depending on what the cookie is used for and how your browser is configured.
Cookies are often classified according to how long they remain active before they expire. A "session" cookie is a file that usually expires when you close your web browser or mobile application. A "permanent" cookie is a file that remains on your device even after you close your browser or mobile application.  Permanent cookies expire according to the expiration date set by us or when you delete them manually.
You can delete or disable cookies through your browser settings.
For more information about this and other key terms that may be relevant, please check out our Glossary of the Privacy Policy.

What types of cookies does Wikimedia use and for what purposes?
Cookies are not required to read or edit WWCO sites. We use the information we receive through cookies and other local data storage technologies to make your interaction with WWCO Sites safer and better, to better understand user preferences and interaction with WWCO Sites, as well as to improve our services in general. Cookies are necessary for logging in and for your changes to be associated with the user account; without cookies, your changes will be anonymous and not associated with the account.
We use cookies, JavaScript, tracking pixels and other local data storage technologies to achieve various purposes. Below is a list of the categories of cookies we use and what they are used for.

Functionality:
These files and cookies help WWCO sites work and are necessary in order for you to navigate the WWCO site and use their functions. These cookies are useful for remembering your username in the login field, maintaining your session and remembering previous actions, saving your login (if selected) and much more.

Preferences:
These cookies store your preferences so that they can be remembered the next time you use WWCO sites for more personalized use.  These cookies are useful for recognizing and saving your language preferences, remembering the changes you have made to the text size, fonts and other display settings so that we can provide you with the desired appearance and much more.

  Performance and Analysis:
These cookies count the number of visitors and collect information about how you use WWCO Sites. This allows us to better understand your user experience on WWCO sites and helps us improve them for you and other users - for example, by making sure that users easily find what they need. Other examples include:
* memorizing the pages visited and actions taken on WWCO sites so that we can optimize the pages;
* remembering whether users receive error messages from web pages;
* save your last read articles directly on your device so that they can be quickly retrieved;
* memorizing search topics so that we can optimize the search results that we provide to you;
* memorizing a list of articles that you follow on your watch list so that we can recommend similar articles that may interest you.

Third-party:
We will never use third-party cookies on our wiki sites unless we receive your permission to do so. These cookies will allow us to provide services provided by third parties, such as like and share buttons. When a third party provides this kind of service, they may need to use cookies to provide their services. If you have ever encountered third-party cookies transmitted to your device during your access to WWCO wiki sites, where you have not taken any action to authorize the use and/or transfer of these cookies (for example, which may have been mistakenly placed by another user or administrator), please inform us about this cookie at mail.wwco@gmail.com.

What are my cookie settings?
Although this is an incomplete list, below are some actions you can take to limit the use of cookies and other local storage technologies on your device. Although cookies and other local storage technologies may not be required to use our sites, some features may not work properly if you disable them.
You can:
* delete or disable certain locally stored data in your browser settings (you can reset your web browser settings to refuse all cookies or specify when a cookie is sent).;
* use a browser that can block third-party cookies; or
* install a plug-in to lock locally stored data, if any.
Disabling browser cookies will prevent tracking pixels from tracking your specific activity. The tracking pixel can still record an anonymous visit from your IP address, but the unique information will not be recorded. If you don't want to receive tracking pixels, you will need to disable HTML images in the browser-based email client, and this may affect your ability to view images in other email messages that you receive.
