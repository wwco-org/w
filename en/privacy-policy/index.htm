Privacy Policy of the WorldWide CDI Organization for its sites and projects

Welcome!

This document is the privacy policy of the Worldwide CDI Organization (WWCO), which applies to all its sites and projects. This Privacy Policy applies to all WWCO websites, without exception, hosted by the WorldWide CDI Organization, unless otherwise specified on the relevant website. The Main Privacy Policy of the CDI World Organization is a separate document, and applies to all websites without exception.

This Privacy Policy applies to our collection and processing of your information, which was obtained by us as a result of your use of the websites. By using these websites, you declare your consent to comply with the terms of this Privacy Policy and terms of use WWCO. If you refuse to comply with the terms of this Privacy Policy and/or our Terms of Use, we ask you to refuse to use these websites.

If you have any questions or suggestions regarding this Privacy Policy for WWCO sites, or regarding our Terms of Use, please contact.

Thank you!

What information we collect and what we do with it

Personal data: non-personally identifiable information

Like most website operators, WWCO collects personal data in the form of non-personally identifiable information, which is usually provided by web browsers and servers, including, but not limited to: browser type, language preferences, the website from which the transition was made, as well as information about the date and time of each website visitor's request. We collect these personal data in order to get an idea of how you interact with these websites and related materials, as well as to support and improve the efficiency of working with such sites and materials, and to protect against malicious user actions. From time to time WWCO has the right to publicly publish the specified personal data in a generalized or depersonalized form, for example, by publishing a report on trends in the use of our websites.

Personal data: identification information

Depending on how you use the website, you may disclose a different amount of personal data with which it is possible to establish your identity (for example, IP address, information about the user's software agent or general location information). We are committed to maintaining the confidentiality of such information, except as provided for in this Privacy Policy for sites not created using the wiki model.

We use this information for our own legitimate interests, including in order to get an idea of how you interact with these non-wiki websites and related materials, as well as to protect against malicious user actions.

If you provide your email address, we have the right to use this address to contact you or to send you surveys in order to get an idea of how you use our services, or to provide you with up-to-date information about the CDI World Organization, projects and work of the organization. Participation in surveys is never mandatory. Each message will contain information on how to unsubscribe from receiving subsequent emails.

Cookies and other data collection tools

We also have the right to use common data storage technologies to obtain information with which you can be identified, including, but not limited to: cookies, data on a local storage device, JavaScript or tracking pixels. We undertake to respect the confidentiality of such information, except in cases where the Privacy Policy for sites not created according to the wiki model provides otherwise. We use this information to improve the security and efficiency of your work with websites created not according to the wiki model, to better understand user preferences and user interaction with websites created not according to the wiki model, as well as to improve the quality of our services. You have the right to delete or disable some or all cookies, as well as data stored on a local storage device in your browser settings, depending on the type of browser you use. Although cookies and data stored on a local storage device may not be a prerequisite for using the above websites, if you disable cookies and/or the data storage function on a local storage device, some functions may not work properly. If we use third-party cookies on such websites, we undertake to provide up-to-date information in Notification of the use of cookies. You can learn more about some of the cookies we use, their expiration dates, as well as the purposes for which we use them here.

Circumstances under which we have the right to transfer your information to third parties

With your permission

We have the right to transfer your information to third parties for certain purposes, subject to your consent to the transfer of such information, or in the cases provided below.

Third-party service organizations

We may share your information (including email address and general location information) with persons with whom we cooperate (for example, third-party service organizations, contractors, volunteers) in order to maintain, improve or better understand the operation of websites created not according to the wiki model, or others WWCO projects and websites. When providing services to us, some third-party service organizations and contractors also have the right to collect and store user information, including email addresses and general location information obtained based on an IP address, in accordance with the privacy policies of such organizations and in accordance with additional requirements stated by us. If necessary, we may provide special protection mechanisms, for example, non-disclosure agreements, in order to guarantee the confidentiality of these third-party service organizations when working with your personal data.

Legal grounds

We have the right to access, store or disclose your personal data if we have reason to believe that this is necessary to comply with the requirements of a valid and legally binding court order, subpoena, court order, legal requirements, regulatory requirements or other judicial or administrative requirements. However, if we have reason to believe that the relevant requirement for the disclosure of user information is invalid or represents a case of abuse of the legal system, and the relevant user has no intention to personally oppose the disclosure of information, we undertake to take all measures to counteract the fulfillment of such a requirement. We undertake to notify you by e-mail no later than 10 (ten) calendar days prior to the date of disclosure of your personal data in response to a legitimate request. However, we will be able to notify you only if there are no legal prohibitions against contacting you, if there is no probable threat of bodily harm that may appear or increase as a result of informing you of such a request, and also provided that you have provided your email address.

The provisions of this Privacy Policy for sites that are not created according to the wiki model do not limit the possibility of filing legal objections or using legal remedies against third-party claims (civil, criminal or governmental) for the disclosure of your data. If you receive a request to disclose your data, we recommend that you immediately contact a legal consultant.

Ensuring your protection, our protection and the protection of third parties

We may need to disclose your personal data if we have reasonable grounds to believe that this is necessary for the following: enforcement or investigation of potential violations of our Terms of Use or this Privacy Policy for sites not created according to the wiki model; investigation and protection of our interests in potential lawsuits or claims; prevention of imminent and serious bodily injury or death of individuals; protection of our organization, employees, contractors, users or the public; identification, prevention or other assessment and neutralization of potential cases of spam, distribution of malicious software, fraud, abuse, illegal actions, threats related to security or technical problems.

Training and research

We have the right to transfer depersonalized or generalized information to researchers, scientists, representatives of the academic community and other interested third parties who plan to study the websites of the CDI World Organization, including these websites that are not created according to the wiki model. Providing this information will help these individuals understand usage scenarios, browsing statistics, demographic statistics, and behavioral patterns. Subsequently, these persons have the right to provide us and our users with their opinions so that we can get a better idea of the work of the websites of the World Organization CDI and improve their effectiveness.

If we provide access to personal information to third-party developers or researchers, we establish a number of requirements for them, for example, the use of reasonable technical and legal protection mechanisms, in order for these organizations to work with this information strictly in accordance with the provisions of this Privacy Policy and according to our instructions. If these developers or researchers subsequently publish their work or conclusions, we ask them not to disclose your personal data. Please note that, despite the fact that we set obligations for developers and researchers, we cannot guarantee that they comply with the agreements reached, nor can we guarantee regular analysis or audit of their projects on our part (for more information about reverse identification, see the Frequently Asked Questions section of WWCO's Privacy Policy).

In general, WWCO does not disclose personal data obtained through websites that are not created using the wiki model, except in the circumstances provided for in this Privacy Policy for sites that are not created using the wiki model.

Information that you transmit to third-party service organizations

Websites that are not created according to the wiki model may provide for the possibility of accessing third-party services through the website interface. For example, if you click on the Share button or on a link contained in a blog entry, you may be redirected to a third-party website, such as a Twitter post or an article in the New York Times. Please note that this Privacy Policy applies exclusively to information that has been collected by us. You are solely responsible for reviewing and understanding the privacy policy and privacy practices of third-party services that you use.

How we protect your data

We are committed to protecting your information from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. We use a number of physical and technical means, policies and procedures (for example, access control procedures, firewalls and physical security mechanisms) to protect our systems and your personal data. Unfortunately, there are no completely secure data transmission or control mechanisms; thus, we cannot guarantee that our security system will never be violated (using technical means or by violating our policies and procedures).

Access to your information

WWCO makes every effort to ensure accurate processing and storage of information. If you plan to access your personal data stored by us, change them, update them, terminate or restrict their use or delete them, declare your objection to the processing of personal data or request an electronic copy of your personal data to transfer such data to another organization (provided that the right to transfer data is provided for by applicable law) or are going to change your preferences regarding communication with WWCO, you (or your authorized representative) can contact us at email. Despite the fact that we will take all reasonable measures to fulfill your request, in some situations your requirements cannot be fully fulfilled (for example, if we cannot properly verify your identity to provide access to personal data, or if we are legally obliged to store information, or do not have the technical ability to change or delete data). Thus, the execution of your request remains at the sole discretion of WWCO.

The retention period of your data

After we have received personal data from you, we undertake to store it for as short a period as possible, in accordance with the requirements of maintaining, understanding and improving the efficiency of sites that are not created according to the wiki model.

Transfer of information to the USA

If you intend to access or use one of these non-wiki websites, both in the United States and outside the United States, you agree to the collection, transfer, storage, processing, disclosure and other scenarios for the use of your data in the United States, as provided for in this Privacy Policy for non-wiki sites. You also declare your consent to the transfer of your information from the United States to the territory of other countries where, in connection with the provision of services to you, different or less stringent data protection laws may apply compared to the relevant laws in your country.

Responding to a request to ban tracking

The protection of non-public information of our users is one of our priorities. In accordance with this Privacy Policy, we have the right to provide your information to third parties only in certain circumstances, more detailed information about which is set out in the section "Circumstances in which we have the right to transfer your information to third parties" of this Privacy Policy.

Since the protection mechanisms of all users are subject to this Privacy Policy for sites that are not created according to the wiki model, we do not change our behavior when the browser signals that data tracking is prohibited.

Changes to this Privacy Policy

Due to the fact that circumstances may change over time, and we intend to ensure that this Privacy Policy complies with the methods of work we use and legal requirements, we reserve the right to periodically make changes to the Privacy Policy. We undertake to inform you in a timely manner and in accordance with the established procedure about changes to this Privacy Policy.

We ask you to study the current version of this Privacy Policy. If you continue to use these websites created not according to the wiki model after the new version of the Privacy Policy comes into force, this will be interpreted as your consent to such a version of the Privacy Policy.

Communication information

If you have any questions or suggestions regarding this Privacy Policy or information collected in accordance with this Privacy Policy, please contact us by email mail.wwco@gmail.com or directly.

Thanks!

Thank you for reading the Privacy Policy for sites created not according to the wiki model. We hope that you will not be disappointed in using WWCO websites, and thank you for participating in the creation, maintenance and constant work on improving the world's largest repository of free knowledge.
