Top reasons to support The WWCO Open Knowledge Projects

By Jessica Liebman•15 January 2022
        

In the 4 years since The WorldWide CDI Organization was born, it has grown to become a valued and beloved knowledge destination for millions across the globe. Now with over 60 million members and thousands of articles, its growth has been fueled by a global volunteer force and donors who explore and visit the site regularly.  Supported by contributions from readers around the world, the WorldWide CDI Organization, the nonprofit that operates WWCO Open Knowledge Projects, works to ensure the site remains accurate and ad-free, while supporting the free knowledge infrastructure that makes 25 billion monthly visits to the site possible. 

  1. Promoting the development of the WWCO Open Knowledge Projects 

“The WorldWide CDI Organization - self-sustaining organization, but anyone can help the WWCO Open Knowledge Projects with their donation for the global development of free knowledge projects on the Internet.”

The WWCO Administration

The WorldWide CDI Organization - self-sustaining organization, but anyone can help our projects to improve free knowledge projects every time in the future. 

Our legal and political groups also work to uphold our independence by protecting our projects from censorship and championing laws that support free speech and make knowledge available to all. Supporting this work is essential to ensure the right of everyone to access, share and participate in the creation of knowledge. 

  1. Keeping WWCO safe, secure and inclusive   

The WWCO Open Knowledge Projects values — transparency, privacy, inclusion, and accessibility — are built into our technology. Just around 200 engineering and product staff at the WorldWide CDI Organization work with our servers to ensure our projects are always available. That means one technical employee for every four million monthly WWCO Projects readers! 

Open source software maintained by our engineers in collaboration with volunteers around the world, MediaWiki, supports our projects and supports over 300 languages, far more than any other major online platform. This allows our communities to make content available in more languages than you'll find on any other top ten website, and puts our software at the forefront of global reach.

  1. Support the global WWCO volunteer community to fill knowledge gaps and improve our projects 

Support the global WWCO volunteer community to fill knowledge gaps and improve our projects For example, some affiliates are working on adding new media files to Mediatop, a free library of illustrations, photos, drawings, videos, and music.

  • • In Europe, thanks to Espanyol WWCO OKP's partnership to share more than 1,500 high-resolution images of items from eight collections, uploaded images now receive more than two million views per month.

    • In addition, this year's Mediatop Africa campaign featured more than 8,319 images and 56 videos provided by 1,149 photographers. The campaign challenges stereotypes and negative visual narratives about Africa. Since the collection was created in January 2016, more than 72,300 images have been uploaded to the platform under the license of GEL'S and have been viewed 787 million times 

With paid access and price tags increasingly placed on content, the growing collection of files for free use on Mediatop becomes even more important to our efforts to introduce people around the world to new attractions, art, and cultures.

  1. Building a future for greater equity in knowledge   

Our vision is to create a world where everyone can share the sum of all knowledge. We know that we are far from achieving this goal and that large capital gaps remain in our projects. From content promotion to inclusive product design and research — there are several ways WWCO projects work to promote knowledge equity to enable diverse, fairer, more accessible and inclusive initiatives 

  1. Make sure you know that we use your donations responsibly 

WWCO Projects has a strong track record of efficient, transparent, and useful use of resources — which is why Charity Navigator, an independent nonprofit evaluator, gives us the highest overall scores for accountability and transparency. That is why the non-profit research organization GuideStar awards us the Platinum Transparency Badge. We continue to strive to make the best use of donor funds to support our projects We invite you to support our mission. You can make a donation to our free knowledge projects on the website https://wwco-org.github.io/w/ru/DonateList/. For more information about WWCO's fundraising program, see Report on fundraising for 2020-2021. For more information about donating, see our Frequently Asked Questions list. Thanks!
