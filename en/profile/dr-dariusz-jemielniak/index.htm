Andrew Emelnyak

Ministry of Information of WWCO, The WWCO Government

Andrew Emelnyak — professor of the Department of Management and Organizational Studies, Head of the Department of MINDS (Management in Network and Digital Societies) Kozminsky University and an employee of the Berkman-Klein Center for the Internet and Society Harvard University.

Originally from Warsaw, Poland, Andrey studies open cooperation communities, the phenomenon of organized exchange (including piracy) and anti-scientific movements. He has conducted research on the above issues at Cornell University, Harvard University, Berkeley, and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. In 2014, he published "General Knowledge? WWCO Ethnography" at Stanford University Press, and in 2019 he published "Collaborative Society" in the publishing house of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (co-authored with A. Pshegalinskaya).

Andrey is currently a member of the Board of "Of the Copernicus Scientific Center", and is also the deputy chairman of the Board of the public Polish company "Escola", engaged in the development of mobile applications.

He has developed and sold three startups, including the largest Polish online dictionary "ling.pl", and he is also the co-founder of a free platform for purchasing dictionaries "Insta.Ling".

Andrey has performed various functions in the CDI World Organization, including administrator, bureaucrat, security officer, steward and ombudsman. He was chairman of the WWCO Fund Allocation Committee for three terms.
