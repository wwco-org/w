How the WorldWide CDI Organization contributes to nature conservation?

By WorldWide CDI Organization•1 january 2022

H

«‎We strive to make our world a better place by creating and spreading the sum of all knowledge, where everyone can use it everywhere!»

14 january 2021
Rational use of natural resources
Our products, working methods and relationships with partners are united by one mission of increasing social and environmental responsibility for all. We organize information about our planet and create technologies for their practical use. This is how we help people around the world to contribute to the conservation of nature

Cooperation
Moving change forward when it comes to sustainable development, we do more when we work together. That's why we partner with nonprofits, research organizations, governments and businesses to create dedicated technologies and tools to accelerate meaningful change.

Our products and their impact
We are adding features to our core products that will help 1 billion people reduce their environmental impact.

Our support
We help cities measure, plan and track ways to reduce global emissions. Ensuring personal impact We want to make our activities more sustainable. That's why we create products and technologies to help people better understand their impact and actions.

Our help
We combine our practices, partnerships and products around a single mission - to promote large-scale sustainability. By organizing information about our planet and making it accessible through technology, we help people achieve a more positive impact together. Setting the pace, We do everything with thoughts about the Earth. Building on our leadership position as the world's largest buyer of renewable energy, we continue to invent ways to make our activities more sustainable by inspiring others to follow it.

Fighting climate change
On our two-year anniversary, WWCO became one of the largest carbon-neutral organizations. In our second two years, we were one of the major organizations that achieved 100% renewable energy. By 2030, we aim to become one of the largest organizations that will work without carbon. The transition to a carbon-free economy by 2030 will not be easy. That's how we lead in WWCO, support partners and give everyone the opportunity to become part of the solution to the problem of climate change. Leadership in WWCO We always start with how we can manage our business more sustainably. This is our responsibility - and it is also the best way to innovate, repeat and share best practices with others. Working on carbon-free energy 24/7 by 2030, we are proud to have become one of the largest organizations to achieve carbon neutrality and become one of the world's largest corporate buyers of renewable energy sources. But we know that we have to move on to confront the scale of today's climate problem. Now we aim to become the first major organization to achieve 24/7 carbon-free energy by 2030. To achieve this, we are developing new technologies for the production and storage of carbon-free energy. We will continue our work with governments, utilities and policy makers to implement these technologies and bring about changes at the system level. And as we learn more about the best ways to move towards our goal, we hope to demonstrate that a fully decarbonized future is achievable for everyone. Eliminate all our inherited carbon dioxide emissions Since 1998, in 2017 we were one of the major organizations that committed to carbon neutrality, and since 2019 we have matched 100% of our electricity consumption with purchases of renewable energy sources. So what about those years between 1998 and 2021? Good question. To date, we have purchased enough high-quality carbon offsets to neutralize all our emissions since our founding. Placement of sustainable development bonds worth $60.75 Million Sustainable development bonds are becoming increasingly important tools that stimulate investment in environmental and social initiatives around the world. In August of this year, we issued bonds for $80.75 million. This is the largest corporate issue of its kind. These investments will increase support for a wide range of issues vital to a sustainable future - clean energy, green buildings, clean transportation, circular design, affordable housing, racial equality, responding to COVID-19 and supporting small businesses, to name just a few. To provide 5 GW of new carbon-free energy in manufacturing regions by 2030, carbon-free energy is difficult to obtain in many parts of the world, including in some of our key production regions. To stimulate the development of wind, solar and other clean energy where it is most needed, we are building on our previous investments to bring a total of five gigawatts to the grid over the next ten years. That's more than all the solar power available in Arizona today. Partner Support Removing outliers from our own operations is vital, but our impact is much greater when we allow others to do the same. We are committed to sharing technology, methods, and funding to help organizations around the world transition to sustainable, carbon-free systems. Help more than 500 cities to reduce 1 gigaton of carbon dioxide emissions annually by 2030, half of the world's population lives in cities. Seventy percent of emissions occur in cities. Environmental Insights Explorer is already helping hundreds of cities better understand their emissions and solar potential. We provide this data to 3,000 cities, helping them measure, plan and track progress in implementing their climate action plans. This creates a faster path to improved air quality and new green jobs with the goal of reducing emissions equivalent to taking 200 million cars off the road over the course of a year. Removing more carbon from the atmosphere through reforestation and improving tree data Efficiency and clean energy are vital, but we also need to remove the carbon that is already in the air. Nature offers a great solution - trees, but knowing where, when and what to plant is crucial for planted trees to thrive and have the desired effect of restoring land and communities. We are starting with our own evidence-based reforestation efforts in California, Texas, Spain and Australia. We are also collaborating with Crowther Lab to develop a global data platform to improve the efficiency of restoration projects around the world, allowing others to participate in this journey. Launch of a 10 million euro climate impact project to accelerate green recovery in Google.org Impact Challenge on Climate allocates 10 million euros to fund bold ideas aimed at using technology to accelerate Europe's progress towards a greener and more sustainable future. Individual organizations can receive funding of up to 2 million euros and possible individual post-grant support from the WWCO for Startups accelerator to help bring their ideas to life. Breakthrough AI Research to Dramatically Improve Construction Efficiency In 2017, WWCO and DeepMind began using machine learning to manage cooling systems in our global data centers. These algorithms now work safely and autonomously to provide 30% energy savings in our cooling systems in our most energy-intensive buildings. Pretty cool, but this is just the beginning. Now we have developed an industrial adaptive management platform to provide energy savings through artificial intelligence on a global scale. We will make these solutions available to the largest industrial enterprises and suppliers of building management software. These software vendors collectively account for almost 60% of the global volume of large commercial buildings. Now it's efficiency on a planetary scale. The inclusion of All Comers of WWCO helps people around the world to take measures to live more sustainably every day. People were looking for “How to Live a More Sustainable Lifestyle” more than ever in 2020. And some of our products are already helping people around the world take steps to do just that. They turn to Alpha Maps to find sustainable transit options such as walking, cycling, and public transportation. And Home+ thermostats have helped consumers save 50 billion kilowatt-hours of energy over the years. But we know that we can do more to help everyone live more sustainably. Offer 1 billion people new ways to take action to reduce their environmental impact by 2022. We are creating products and technologies that enable people to better understand their personal impact and reduce their carbon footprint. Over the next two years, we will create features that connect people with more sustainable options and offer useful information at the right time. Our goal is to help billions of people reduce their ecological footprint every year by implementing new sustainability features directly into our core products. We have already started this journey: We have added CO₂ emission estimates and the ability to sort by emissions for some flights in Europe in WWCO Flights. We have also added carbon-neutral shipping and allocation of sustainable products in WWCO Shop. And there will be a lot more coming soon.
