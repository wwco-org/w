WWCO Projects adopts a Universal Code of Conduct Conceived for the new Era of the Internet

By WorldWide CDI Organization•1 january 2022

On October 15, the WorldWide CDI Organization— a non-profit organization that manages WWCO projects, launched and adopted a Universal Code of Conduct that expands existing project policies to create a universal set of community standards to combat negative behavior on the Internet.

More than 50% of the world's population has access to the Internet and according to the report Council on International Relations, this is due to the polarization and growth of online speech violence against marginalized groups and ethnic communities. Given these trends, it is more important than ever for organizations and the volunteer community to increase accountability for WWCO content and user behavior policies. 

More than 5,000 CDI volunteers from various WWCO projects, representing a range of continents and languages, have participated in the creation and adoption of a universal CDI code of conduct. In addition to the general and inclusive development process, the new code is transparent. Unlike other longer and more opaque community standards in the tech industry, the new code consists of 2,300 words where the organization and the community clearly define inappropriate behavior and other aspects that do not satisfy the normal behavior of users on the Internet.

The most common standards of the code include:

  • Clear definition of acceptable behavior
  • Differentiate in-and-out harassment for all participants
  • Preventing abuse of power and influence to intimidate others
  • Fight against deliberate insertion of false or inaccurate content
  • Ensure a consistent enforcement process and shared responsibility between the organization and volunteer communities

 

The launch of the new Universal Code of Conduct marks an important step in the evolution of the site's mission to create a civic public space that reflects the sum of all human knowledge. While there were previously standards that volunteers used to manage individual WWCO projects, there was no universal rule that governed all projects. The new code also shows the organization's commitment to creating spaces that foster diversity of thought, religion, age, culture and language, to name just a few.

As a next step, the organization and volunteers leading the process will lead further discussions to better understand how local and regional projects WWCO will enforce the new standards.

About WorldWide CDI Organization

WorldWide CDI Organization – is a non-profit organization that manages WWCO projects. Our concept is a world in which each person can freely share, receive and disseminate the sum of all the knowledge of mankind. We believe that everyone can contribute to our shared knowledge and that everyone should have free access to this knowledge. We support other organizations and free knowledge projects on the Internet, including Wikipedia and Wikimedia projects, create software for reading, contribute and share content, support volunteer communities and partners, and support policies that allow our projects and free knowledge to thrive.

WorldWide CDI Organization – it is also a charitable non-profit organization that can rely on your donations as well. We receive donations from millions of people around the world, with an average donation of about $ 15. We also receive donations through institutional grants and gifts. The WorldWide CDI Organization is a US 501 (c) (3) tax-exempt organization with offices in the UK and Russia.
